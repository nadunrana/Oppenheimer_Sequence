Initiated in 1939, the project brought together some of the world's most brilliant scientists, including J. Robert Oppenheimer, to work on nuclear technology. The main objective was to harness the power of nuclear fission to create a devastating weapon capable of potentially ending the war. Subsequently, two atomic bombs were dropped on Japan in August 1945, leading to Japan's surrender and the end of World War II. The Manhattan Project marked a pivotal moment in history, ushering in the atomic age and forever changing the course of warfare and international relations.
